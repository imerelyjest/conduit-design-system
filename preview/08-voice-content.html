Brand · voice & content

Plainspoken. Specific. No exclamation points.

Conduit sounds like a contractor who's done this before — direct, confident, allergic to enterprise jargon. The work is the subject. Every claim has a number behind it.

01

Direct

Sentence-case headings. The verb is doing the work. We start clauses with subjects, not buzzwords.

02

Specific

30 minutes, 36–48 hours, 1–2 weeks. Never "fast", "soon", "shortly", "quickly".

03

Plainspoken

"We build" beats "we leverage". "Around how you actually work" beats "tailored to your unique needs".

04

Confident

No hedging, no exclamation points, no "we'd love to". State the offer, name the timeframe, mean it.

Don'tBuzzword stack

  • "Transformative CRM solutions for your business needs."
  • "We leverage cutting-edge AI to deliver next-generation value."
  • "Schedule a discovery consultation to begin your journey."
  • "Tailored solutions, unparalleled support, world-class outcomes!"
  • "Empowering small businesses through digital transformation."

DoPlainspoken

  • "Your sales pipeline, automated."
  • "AI-fast first drafts, finished by people who care."
  • "Book a 30-minute call. We reply within 48 hours."
  • "Built around how your business actually works."
  • "Custom software for owner-operated businesses in DFW."
Specificity over promises

Numbers, not adjectives.

Every commitment in our copy has a number behind it. If you can't put a unit on the end, rewrite the line.
